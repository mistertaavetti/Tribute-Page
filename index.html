Abiodun "Ebenezer Williams" Aloba

A pioneer of the Nigerian press

Who Was He?

Abiodun Aloba (1921-2001) was a Nigerian journalist from Edo State, Nigeria who was an editor in the Times Group of Newspapers and later became the general manager of the Nigerian Observer. He was one of the founding members of the Zikist Movement in 1946 (a radical political group founded in February 1946 by young enthusiastic Nigerian nationalists). He was later appointed Special Adviser to Former Nigerian President, Alhaji Shehu Shagari.
Aloba wrote a few booklets and insightful articles on Nigeria.

“In the years to come, his work in other newspapers would make him the best known Nigerian journalist of his generation. His Sunday Times column under the anonym of Ebenezer Williams, was especially admired for its sardonic style.”

-- Former Nigerian President, Alhaji Shehu Shagari
